# LGPD (Brazil), POPIA (South Africa), and DPA 2018 (UK) Audit Prompts

This collection provides comprehensive audit checkpoint prompts for LGPD (Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados), POPIA (Protection of Personal Information Act), and UK DPA 2018 compliance assessments.

## Context and Background

### LGPD (Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados)
**Effective Date**: September 18, 2020 (with 2-year transition period)  
**Scope**: All organizations processing personal data of individuals in Brazil  
**Key Principles**: Lawfulness, Loyalty, Transparency, Security, Prevention, Non-discrimination, Accountability  
**Penalties**: Up to 2% of global revenue, fines up to R$ 50 million  
**Data Subject Rights**: Access, Correction, Deletion, Portability, Opposition, Information about processing

### POPIA (Protection of Personal Information Act)
**Effective Date**: July 1, 2021 (with phased implementation)  
**Scope**: Processing of personal information of data subjects in South Africa  
**Key Principles**: Accountability, Processing Limitation, Purpose Specification, Further Processing Limitation, Information Quality, Openness, Security Safeguards, Data Subject Participation  
**Penalties**: Fines up to 10 million Rand or 10 years imprisonment  
**Data Subject Rights**: Access, Correction, Deletion, Objection, Restriction

### DPA 2018 (UK Data Protection Act)
**Effective Date**: May 25, 2018 (post-Brexit implementation)  
**Scope**: Processing of personal data in the UK, implementing GDPR with UK-specific provisions  
**Key Principles**: Lawful processing, Purpose limitation, Data minimization, Accuracy, Storage limitation, Integrity, Confidentiality, Accountability  
**Penalties**: Up to £17.5 million or 4% of global turnover  
**Data Subject Rights**: Access, Rectification, Erasure, Restriction, Portability, Objection, Automated decision-making

## Regulatory Context
- **LGPD**: Brazil's comprehensive data protection law, inspired by GDPR but with local adaptations
- **POPIA**: South Africa's data protection framework, replacing outdated sectoral laws
- **DPA 2018**: UK's post-Brexit data protection regime, maintaining GDPR alignment while allowing UK-specific rules
