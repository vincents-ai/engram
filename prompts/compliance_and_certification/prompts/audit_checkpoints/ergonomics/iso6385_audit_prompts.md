## Context and Background

### ISO 6385:2016 Ergonomics Principles in the Design of Work Systems
**Effective Date**: December 1, 2016 (current version)  
**Scope**: Design and evaluation of work systems for human use  
**Key Principles**: Systems approach, user-centered design, iterative design, holistic perspective  
**Structure**: Principles for work system design and evaluation  
**Benefits**: Improved work efficiency, enhanced safety, better user satisfaction, reduced fatigue and errors

## Regulatory Context
- **ISO 6385**: Fundamental ergonomics principles for work systems
- **Work System Definition**: Includes people, tools, technology, tasks, environment
- **Human Factors Integration**: Considers physical, cognitive, and organizational aspects
- **Design Focus**: Proactive ergonomics in system development

## ISO 6385 Work System Design Assessment

**Work System Components:**
- **People**: Skills, experience, physical characteristics, cognitive abilities
- **Technology**: Tools, equipment, software, automation systems
- **Tasks**: Work methods, procedures, job design, workload distribution
- **Environment**: Physical workspace, lighting, noise, climate, safety conditions
- **Organization**: Management structure, communication, work organization

**Ergonomics Design Criteria:**
- **Health and Safety**: Prevention of injuries, fatigue, and health issues
- **Performance**: Efficiency, productivity, quality of work
- **Comfort**: Physical comfort, mental well-being, job satisfaction
- **Usability**: Ease of use, learnability, error prevention
