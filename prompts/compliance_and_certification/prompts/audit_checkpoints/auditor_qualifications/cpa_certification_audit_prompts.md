This collection provides comprehensive audit checkpoint prompts for verifying CPA certification compliance and auditor qualifications for financial auditing.

## Context and Background

### CPA (Certified Public Accountant)
**Issuing Body**: State Boards of Accountancy (US), equivalent bodies internationally  
**Effective Date**: 1896 (ongoing certification program)  
**Scope**: Financial auditing, accounting, financial reporting, assurance services  
**Requirements**: Education + examination + experience + ethics  
**Renewal**: Varies by jurisdiction, typically annual CPE requirements  
**Relevance**: Required for SOX, financial statement audits, Basel III, regulatory compliance

## Regulatory Context
- **State Board Standards**: Varies by jurisdiction but follows uniform standards
- **CPE Requirements**: Continuing professional education mandatory
- **Code of Ethics**: Professional conduct and independence requirements
- **Global Recognition**: Accepted worldwide for financial audit qualifications

**CPA Compliance Assessment:**

1. **Certification Status Verification**
   - Evaluate CPA certification validity and expiration date
   - Assess certification number and registration confirmation
   - Review certification maintenance requirements
   - Validate certification renewal status

2. **Education Requirements Assessment**
   - Assess 150 semester hours of education requirement
   - Review education documentation and transcripts
   - Validate education relevance to accounting profession
   - Confirm education quality and accreditation

3. **Examination Requirements Verification**
   - Evaluate Uniform CPA Examination completion and passing
   - Assess examination sections (AUD, BEC, FAR, REG)
   - Review examination preparation and study methods
   - Validate examination retake history if applicable

4. **Experience Requirements Assessment**
   - Evaluate experience requirements (varies by jurisdiction)
   - Assess experience quality and relevance
   - Review experience documentation and verification
   - Validate experience progression and development

5. **Continuing Professional Education (CPE)**
   - Assess CPE requirements (varies by jurisdiction, typically 40-80 hours/year)
   - Review CPE activity documentation and quality
   - Validate CPE relevance to accounting profession
   - Confirm CPE reporting accuracy and timeliness

6. **Code of Ethics Compliance**
   - Evaluate adherence to AICPA Code of Professional Conduct
   - Assess independence and objectivity in audit engagements
   - Review conflict of interest management
   - Validate professional conduct and integrity
