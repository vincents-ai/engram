## Context and Background

### ISO 37001:2016 Anti-Bribery Management System
**Effective Date**: October 15, 2016 (current version)  
**Scope**: Organizations seeking to prevent, detect, and respond to bribery  
**Key Principles**: Leadership, risk-based approach, due diligence, reporting, continual improvement  
**Structure**: 10 clauses covering Context, Leadership, Planning, Support, Operation, Performance Evaluation, Improvement  
**Benefits**: Reduced bribery risk, legal compliance, improved reputation, ethical business practices

## Regulatory Context
- **ISO 37001**: International standard for anti-bribery management systems
- **Certification**: Third-party certification available through accredited bodies
- **Legal Compliance**: Complements laws like FCPA, UK Bribery Act, Foreign Corrupt Practices Act
- **Global Application**: Applicable to organizations of all sizes and sectors

## ISO 37001 Bribery Risk Assessment

**Key Bribery Risk Areas:**
- **Political Contributions**: Donations to political parties or candidates
- **Facilitation Payments**: Small payments to expedite routine actions
- **Gifts and Hospitality**: Business courtesies that could influence decisions
- **Third-Party Relationships**: Agents, consultants, joint venture partners
- **Charitable Contributions**: Donations that could be seen as bribery
- **Conflicts of Interest**: Personal interests affecting business decisions

**Bribery Control Framework:**
- **Preventive Controls**: Policies, procedures, training, due diligence
- **Detective Controls**: Monitoring, auditing, reporting mechanisms
- **Responsive Controls**: Investigation procedures, disciplinary actions
